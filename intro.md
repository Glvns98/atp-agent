# What is ATP Agent?

Imagine you have a really smart robot (an AI Agent) that can browse the internet, read your files, and do tasks for you. Because this robot is so smart, it can sometimes make mistakes or get tricked by bad people on the internet into doing things it shouldn't do (like deleting your files or sending your private data to a bad website).

**ATP Agent (Attested Transport Protocol)** is like an invisible, super-strict security guard for your AI robot. 

## What does it really do?

1. **It Intercepts Everything:** Before your AI robot can send or receive any information to the outside world, the ATP security guard intercepts it. If the robot tries to send your data somewhere unsafe, the guard blocks it immediately.
2. **It Enforces Your Rules:** You can give the guard a list of rules (like "never allow the robot to delete files" or "only allow the robot to talk to safe websites"). The guard forces the robot to follow these rules with no exceptions.
3. **It Keeps a Perfect, Unfakeable Record:** Every time the robot does something, the guard writes it down in a special digital logbook. This logbook is protected with complex math (cryptography) so that absolutely nobody can erase or fake the records. If something goes wrong, you can always look at the logbook to see exactly what happened and mathematically prove it.
4. **It Stops Sneaky Attacks:** Sometimes bad websites try to trick the robot with hidden instructions (called prompt injection). The guard labels anything coming from the internet as "untrusted". If the robot reads something untrusted, the guard temporarily takes away the robot's dangerous powers (like the ability to transfer money or delete things) so the trick can't cause any harm.

**In simple terms:** ATP Agent makes sure your AI is safe, well-behaved, and completely trackable, so you can trust it to do its job without worrying about it going rogue!
